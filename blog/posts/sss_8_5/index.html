SSS#8.5 - Why I quit pixel art

(This article is not about game dev. It is an edited version of a personal letter.)

TO: ****

I am still a little concerned about my refusal to accept your invitation. I feel like I was being a little mean when I made the statement that “I don’t trust your boss.” The truth is that I have completely lost all desire to create a background in pixel art.

I spent my late teens and early twenties studying to become an animation background artist. At one point I was studying with paper and opaque watercolor (which I probably never told you about). Then, after various setbacks, I escaped painting at normal resolution and found my way in the pixel art world for a while. However, as I continued to do so, the more detail and the higher the resolution. I thought, “There is no reason to be concerned with pixel art anymore…”. After that, I tried drawing with paper and ink, and finally settled on the usual digital painting style.

Pixel art was nothing more than an escape for me, and I might offend some enthusiasts if I asserted this, but perhaps the fact is that it was. Pixel art is beautiful and fun to make, and it was a way for me to escape the stresses of life. But at some point, the question came to me, “Isn’t this just wasting my life?” I understand that my life is not important to the world or to you, but my life is important to me. That is why I quit pixel art. The above is just a long list of excuses. I just say that I did not have the talent for pixel art. I don’t intend to ask for your complete understanding, but I wrote this to let you know how I feel, even if only a little.
